7. POLICY IMPLICATIONS
================================================================================

1. GOVERNANCE IS THE PRIMARY LEVER
   • Civic space and rule of law explain ~50-75% of AWPRI variance
   • Animal welfare improvements require institutional capacity building
   • Technology alone (AI, precision farming) cannot offset governance deficits

2. AI GOVERNANCE URGENCY
   • L3 scores are highest globally, suggesting AI is actively amplifying risk
   • Countries with weak L3 (most of BRICS, SE Asia) lack frameworks to
     oversee AI in livestock operations
   • Recommend immediate AI ethics/oversight frameworks for animal welfare

3. BIFURCATING GLOBAL SYSTEM
   • Western nations showing structural improvement (regulatory tightening)
   • Non-OECD nations showing structural worsening (production + lax oversight)
   • Without North-South technology transfer + capacity building, disparity
     will widen by 2030

4. PRIORITY INTERVENTIONS (by impact):
   a. Build governance capacity in high-risk countries (India, China, Vietnam)
   b. Establish AI ethics frameworks covering non-human stakeholders
   c. Harmonize welfare standards in trade agreements
   d. Support research in tropical climate welfare (heat stress in aquaculture)
