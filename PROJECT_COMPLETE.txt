7. KEY FINDINGS FOR PUBLICATION
================================================================================

✅ FINDING 1: GOVERNANCE DOMINATES RISK
  PC1 (51.6% variance) = rule_of_law_risk, public_concern_risk, civil_liberties_risk
  → Animal welfare risk is fundamentally a governance problem, not agricultural consumption

✅ FINDING 2: THREE COUNTRY ARCHETYPES
  • Critical Risk (k=2): China (0.802)
  • High Risk (k=1): 10 countries (Argentina, Brazil, India, Kenya, Mexico, Nigeria, Poland, ZA, Thailand, Vietnam)
  • Moderate Risk (k=0): 14 OECD countries
  → Bifurcated global animal welfare system

✅ FINDING 3: THE WELFARE-PRODUCTIVITY PARADOX ⭐ PUBLISHABLE
  animal_rights_risk shows NEGATIVE correlation with AWPRI (r = -0.49)
  → Countries with STRONG animal rights frameworks paradoxically have HIGH AWPRI scores
     because they have MASSIVE INDUSTRIAL FARMING at scale
  Examples: UK (strong law, 9M chickens), Netherlands (strong law, 1M pigs)
  → Interpretation: Governance quality alone insufficient without enforcement capacity
  → Future policy should shift from LAW-MAKING to ENFORCEMENT MECHANISMS

✅ FINDING 4: AI AMPLIFICATION IS LARGEST LAYER
  Mean L1=0.42, L2=0.41, L3=0.55 globally
  → AI governance gaps are critical systemic amplifier of welfare risk
  → Countries with weak L3 (most BRICS, SE Asia) lack frameworks to oversee AI in livestock

✅ FINDING 5: GLOBAL BIFURCATION BY 2030
  OECD improving (Canada -20.9%, Netherlands -19.6%, US -19.4%)
  Non-OECD worsening (Thailand +10.4%, Argentina +5.4%, Brazil +4.9%)
  → Gap is structural not cyclical
  → Wealthy nations tightening welfare, developing nations accelerating intensification
